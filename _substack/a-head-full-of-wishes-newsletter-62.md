## My record collection [367] Luna - A Sentimental Education / A Place of Greater Safety (2xCD)

In 2017 the reformed Luna released an album of covers and an EP of instrumentals. For the records they were treated as two separate releases but they were packaged together for the CD and this is that release.

{% ahfowimage "https://media.fullofwishes.co.uk/00-misc/my-record-collection/luna-sentimental-safety-2cd.jpg" "Luna - A Sentimental Education / A Place of Greater Safety" %}

If you read my previous posts on the separate vinyl releases you'll have noticed that I was a little... cool on them (I even, rather shamefully in retrospect, expressed that coolness to Dean face-to-face when he and Britta visited me at The National Archives.

You can read them on the site (the links will be at the bottom of the page) but I'll pull out a couple of quotes just so you're aware of my _coolness_.

<blockquote>
I almost never listen to A Place of Greater Safety - it’s OK I guess but… it feels like a record full of soundtrack cues (a couple of which are), or unfinished songs - they’re fine but Luna’s discography could easily have lived without it - and the same goes for the covers album.
</blockquote>

<blockquote>
So… while I still think that maybe the decision to release a covers album was questionable I think the execution, and therefore the finished product, was flawless so, I guess I’ll shut up about it now.
</blockquote>

Of course despite my whining I wasn't really disappointed and I don't really object to them being in Luna's discography. Also, I did listen to the covers album a lot on release... I must have done because listening now it doesn't feel like an album I was unfamiliar with.

The album also produced a couple of videos - the first features Rose McGowan and was (mostly) shot during a special show Luna performed at Gold Diggers in Hollywood in July 2017.

{% ahfowvideo "RK2YatQ9S1A" "Luna - 'Portrait of Rose' (Fire in Cairo)" %}

The second is a sweet video of schoolyard love shot by Matthew Buzzell... which is additionally sweet because of the Luna at school clips inlcuding schoolboy Sean wearing grown-up Sean's T-shirt (and is that a Bewitched era red jacket on schoolboy Dean?)

{% ahfowvideo "B99f4f8lf4c" "Luna - Friends" %}

{% ahfowimage "https://media.fullofwishes.co.uk/02-luna/photos/joakim/Sean.jpg" "Keep an eye out for Sean's T" %}

The album used Pledgemusic shortly before they went under. I understand that bands and the industry were trying to find their feet in an age where people didn't have to buy records any more but I'm not sure that crowd-funding, and bonus socks was really the way to do that. Although, it was quite nice to see the album taking shape through videos and photos.

{% ahfowvideo "-9mhU2D1BUY" "Luna recording A Sentimental Education, 2017" "https://media.fullofwishes.co.uk/00-misc/my-record-collection/luna-recording-a-sentimental-education.png" %}

The discs came in a lovely tri-fold package and was signed by all four members since it was part of my deluxe Pledgemusic package.

## My record collection [368] Shonen Knife - Genki Shock (CD)

Dean guesting on one track on the always wonderful Shonen Knife's 2006 album Genki Shock might have easily slipped me by...

{% ahfowimage "https://media.fullofwishes.co.uk/00-misc/my-record-collection/shonen-knife-genki-shock-cd.jpg" "Shonen Knife - Genki Shock" %}

... fortunately video of Dean recording his vocal while Luna were in Japan on their final tour was one of the 'deleted scenes' clips on the DVD of Tell Me Do You Miss Me

{% ahfowvideo "t0E6rSi4hEE" "Shonen Knife and Dean Wareham recording Under My Pillow" "https://media.fullofwishes.co.uk/00-misc/my-record-collection/shonen-knife-dean-recording.jpg" %}

The next bit of this post is lifted from a Substack article from last year so apologies if it seems familiar!

<blockquote>
<p>
    Shockingly, for a band as long-established as Shonen Knife I only got to see them live when they played in Sunderland last year. I’m genuinely surprised that in that all of those years living in London and seeing multiple gigs a week that they and I had somehow managed to avoid each other. So, it was quite important that I took the opportunity to fill that gap.
</p>

<p>
    I can honestly say that I have never seen a band enjoy themselves as much as Shonen Knife seem to. They’ve been on this tour for months, and are still the smiley-est band I’ve ever seen… they also rock, much harder than I expected. I mean they were, at times, so close to a metal band that I started to regret my lack of hair. They threw poses, they did the sign of the horns, they came to the front of the stage and head-banged. OK… Raven and Saxon and Girlschool didn’t sing songs about Banana Chips, or Cookie Day, or the power of sweet candy, but the way it made me feel was the same!
</p>

<p>
    They played an hour or so of this beautiful, relentless, power-pop-punk-metal, finished with their lovely cover of The Carpenters Top of the World and left us to go and smile on Glasgow.
</p>

<p>
    Now, they were all so smiley … but not equally so… Risa, played an hour of full-on rock drumming, including occasionally while on her feet, sang a couple of songs, and never stopped looking like she couldn’t believe how wonderful everything is! When she became a member of Shonen Knife 10 years ago she wrote on Facebook that she’d “like to play the drums powerfully and cheerfully with a smile” - achievement unlocked!
</p>

</blockquote>

## This week's anniversaries

### 11th July 2017 - Luna at Gold Digger's in LA

Luna played a private show in LA to film the video for their cover of Fire in Cairo - Josh was kind enough to go along and write it up for AHFoW:

<blockquote>
<p>
    With a 9pm show time, I arrived at 8:15pm and there was already a nice line down the block. In fact when the video of a track off the new album is released you'll see me in that line. The film crew shot us with a vintage Super-8 Bolex camera a few times entering the club. At the door, there was a sign alerting all who entered that a video shoot was taking place inside and we may be included in the filming. Anyone not wishing to be on camera should not enter. Fat chance. Everyone entered.
</p>
<p>...</p>
<p>For their encore, someone in the video crew finally took care of the white hot bright lights set up around the room for the video turned them off and took them away. Instantly the vibe in the room changed. Suddenly there was a mood, a presence, and a feeling rather than just blinding white light/white heat. (How the band managed to perform in that light is beyond me.) Familiar strains of Slide and 23 Minutes in Brussels ended the night. That familiar pent up; ramped up, crescendo took you places far outside Gold Diggers to places elsewhere &endash; the stratosphere, a late-night drive, times gone by. And like that, it was over.</p>
</blockquote>

### 16th July 1998 - Luna at Castle Clinton, Battery Park, NYC

Jan from the mailing list went along to this and reported back for the list:

<blockquote>
<p>
    Luna went on promptly at 7pm. I thought it was damned good.  Luna played
    for about 1.5 hours.   It was a fairly intimate show.  It was the first
    time I've seen them with Lee Wall behind the kit.  His style is different
    than Stanley's.  More syncopated.  More hard snaps on the snare.  But he's
    good.  And he looks like he's having fun and is part of the band.  
    I know someone will want to know what Dean was wearing.  White
    jeans.  Blue checked button down shirt. Tucked in.   Justin is still
    bald.  Lee is still bald.  Sean was wearing an "Air Miami" T-shirt.
</p>

<p>
    Dean played a lot of solos throughout the set.  He may have actually
    played more leads than Sean.   Luna has that ever relaxed yet tight sound
    live.  Around the third song dean said "I feel like I'm in one of those
    dreams where your naked..."  looking a bit wierded out by the small &
    intimate still sunlit scene.  Justin then quipped "you need therapy..."
</p>
</blockquote>
<p class="caption">Jan on the Galaxie 500 Mailing List - Fri 17 Jul 1998</p>
