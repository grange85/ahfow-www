This week I had another flying visit down south to visit my dad for his 86th birthday. I can get from my door in Whitley Bay to my dad's in Arundel in six hours or so most of which is sat on trains listening to music most of which was "comfort listening" - The Beatles, Fairport Convention, The Zombies, Squeeze - so much of my listening these days seems to be of the _comfort_ variety. Is that just more signs of me drifting to small c conservatism?

It doesn't help that last week we turned off (almost certainly temporarily) our access to music streaming services since we have been finding them all so frustrating. The last one to get switched off was Qobuz which unquestionably has the very worst search I've encountered. Amazom Music wasn't much better, Spotify is too tied in with the rubbish that isn't music, Apple will never get any (more) of my money because they annoy the crap out of me, I can't remember what made us give up on Tidal but we did.

Now I know there are many issues for creators around streamed music - [Damon's secondary (possibly primary) career](https://dadadrummer.substack.com/) is involved in trying to improve that but there are also issues for the consumer, and obviously some cross-over between the two.

A few years ago I digitised most of my collection so have my own streaming music server sitting under some shelves in the hall and while the 30,000+ tracks is nowhere near the millions offered by the others the quality is much, much higher! However it becoming my primary service has surfaced some music I haven't listened to for ages, some I have forgotten about, and a surprising amount I have no recollection of. As well as the comfort food I have recently listened to [Joker's Daughter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joker%27s_Daughter_(band)), [The Kingsbury Manx](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Kingsbury_Manx), [Sidi Bou Said](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sidi_Bou_Said_(band)), and [Lotti Golden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lotti_Golden), none of whom have troubled my ears for many years.
