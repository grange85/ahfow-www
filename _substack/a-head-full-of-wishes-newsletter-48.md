### My record collection [339] Dean Wareham - Dean Wareham (test/promo LP)

This is a test pressing of Dean's eponymous album released in 2014 and was sent to me by Nat at Sonic Cathedral, slipped in with the copy of the Lorelle Meets The Obsolete LP "Chambers" that I had ordered.

{% ahfowimage "https://media.fullofwishes.co.uk/00-misc/my-record-collection/dean-wareham-dean-wareham-test-pressing.jpg" "Dean Wareham - Dean Wareham (test pressing)" %}

The LP came in a plain white inner sleeve stamped with the manufacturer details and with the catalogue number written on it in biro. It had plain white labels with the words "Only for Promotion" stamped onto it... which suggests it's a promo rather than a test pressing?

There are a lot of nice performance clips of Dean and his band from this time doing the promo rounds.

{% ahfowvideo "3f-WsWnChnU" "Dean Wareham - The Dancer Disappears (KCRW)" "https://media.fullofwishes.co.uk/00-misc/my-record-collection/dean-wareham-kcrw-screencap-2014.jpg" %}

{% ahfowvideo "PiuKrkOIl4Q" "Dean Wareham - Happy and Free (KEXP)" %}

{% ahfowvideo "r2lVXdSsxQ4" "Dean Wareham - Heartless People (WFUV)" %}

{% ahfowvideo "SEs33Toaseg" "Dean Wareham - Holding Pattern (Amoeba)" %}

As much as I love Dean's more recent albums this is the one that I go back to most.

### My record collection [340] Luna - Penthouse (MC)

This is the fourth and final appearance of Luna's Penthouse and is a cassette copy that I bought (online) from [Sound it Out Records](https://sounditoutrecords.co.uk/) back in 2013 at the same time that I bought the [cassette copy of Lunapark](https://www.fullofwishes.co.uk/2025/01/02/my-record-collection-luna-lunapark-elektra-cassette//2025/01/02/my-record-collection-luna-lunapark-elektra-cassette/?utm_medium=substack&utm_source=social&utm_campaign=newsletter+2026-04-03).

{% ahfowimage "https://media.fullofwishes.co.uk/00-misc/my-record-collection/luna-penthouse-cassette.jpg" "Luna - Penthouse (MC)" %}

Since this is the last appearance it's probably time to look at the tracklisting, not it any way in-depth just with some little snippets that pop into my head... I can't guarantee that these are actually all correct or true.

 - **Chinatown** was written "at least partially" about Terry Tolkin the A&R man who signed the band to Elektra and released Dean and Luna tracks on his own No.6 Records. 
<blockquote>
He enjoyed his new life. He drank and partied and showed up late to work most days, or worked from home. His new life inspired the lyrics I wrote for "Chinatown".
</blockquote>
<p class="caption">Dean Wareham - Black Postcards (2008)</p>

 - **Sideshow by the Seashore** was a venue in Coney Island - the song contains Justin playing a theremin.
 - **Moon Palace** is named after a Paul Auster book and also references "Christopher Boyce" who was The Falcon in the film _The Falcon and the Snowman_.
 - **Double Feature** gave it's name to Dean and Britta's record label.
 - **23 Minutes in Brussels** is probably Luna's most popular song - it consistently topped the annual survey, and a lyric was used as the title of their farewell tour rockumentary, and was played over the end credits. They first played it live in late 1994 although it changed quite a lot before it reached Penthouse. There's an mp3 on the [post about the 23 Minutes single](https://www.fullofwishes.co.uk/2025/03/03/my-record-collection-luna-hedgehog-23-minutes-in-brussels-7/?utm_medium=substack&utm_source=social&utm_campaign=newsletter+2026-04-03).
 - **Lost in Space** opens side two of the LP although Dean admitted in the [Life of the Record podcast](https://lifeoftherecord.com/luna-notes) that they "didn’t plan this as side one and side two because this is the CD era, the compact disc era. We just thought of it as sequencing the ten songs." 
 - I'm not sure I have a smippet of trivia about **Rhythm King** so here are Luna soundchecking it in 2016:
 {% ahfowvideo "crS8VFvP6jY" "Luna "Rhythm King" 2016 09 29 Alexandria VA soundcheck" %}
 - **Kalamazoo**'s origins are in a short film made by Dean's then wife Claudia that I've not managed to track down but has Wally Shawn and (the late) Adrienne Shelley in it.
 - **Hedgehog** was picked to be a (radio) single and a one-track CD single was made as a radio promo... I don't have a copy... but it was also coupled with 23 Minutes in Brussels for the _proper_ single on Beggars Banquet.
 - **Freakin' and Peakin'** is a great finish to the album... but most people probably didn't hear it as a finish since the CD has a bonus track... but since I'm listening to the cassette this is where I'll finish!

Here's Luna playing Freakin' and Peakin' in Atlanta in 2015:
