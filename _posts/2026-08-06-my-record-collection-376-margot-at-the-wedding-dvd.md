Another Noah Baumbach film so, of course, another Dean Wareham contribution. This time with Britta as "music consultants" and also Dean & Britta's Northern Blue appears in the film.

{% ahfowimage "url" "caption" %}

Also... another Noah Baumbach film so another film I just don't get. In my post on [The Squid and the Whale]() I say that "I don’t understand the characters and the way they behave [...] all I see is horrible people behaving horribly to one another" and Margot at the Wedding dials that up to eleven. I must admit I do feel a bit bad not liking these films but they are so unlikeable and Margot at the Wedding is probably the most unlikeable of Noah's films.

Sorry to be repeating what I said about The Squid and the Whale but I genuinely think there must be some regional disconnect at play here, or maybe a social one. There's something about the way these people behave that makes absolutely no sense to me.

I do like the way the film has been made, the movement of the actors, and the camera. The way people interact makes it interesting as a visual spectacle... and the acting can't be faulted - and that is quite a cast that has been assembled here. But it's not a film I can like and not one I'd want to see again.

I haven't as yet managed to track down a copy of the soundtrack album but do have a download of it and it certainly has the fingerprints of the film's "music consultants" on it. As well as Dean & Britta's "Northern Blue" it has Evie Sands "One Fine Summer Morning" and Donavan's "Teen Angel" both of which have been covered by Dean and/or Britta.
