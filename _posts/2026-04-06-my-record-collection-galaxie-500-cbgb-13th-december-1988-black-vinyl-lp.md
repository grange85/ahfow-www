Back at the beginning of the year Damon & Naomi played three genuinely sensational shows at Cafe OTO in London ([I wrote about them here](/2026/01/07/three-nights-with-damon-and-naomi-in-london/) but it deserves a re-write), this copy of Galaxie 500's recently released live LP was acquired at the second of those shows.

{% ahfowimage "https://media.fullofwishes.co.uk/00-misc/my-record-collection/galaxie-500-cbgb-black-vinyl.jpg" "Galaxie 500 - CBGB 13th December 1988 (black LP)" %}

After the show was finished I was loitering by the merch desk, chatting with Naomi and lending my pen out for signings. I mentioned to Naomi that [the "ultra clear and black cloudy" vinyl copy](/2025/08/14/my-record-collection-galaxie-500-cbgb-13th-december-1988/) I had bought on release was horrid and looked like a five year old had scribbled on it&dagger; and said I wanted to buy one of the black vinyl copies they were selling at the show. 

{% ahfowimage "https://media.fullofwishes.co.uk/00-misc/my-record-collection/galaxie-500-cbgb-13th-december-1988-vinyl-mess.jpg" "records should never be this ugly" %}

Naomi insisted on giving me a copy.

OK, it doesn't come with a poster, and a single, and doesn't come in a fancy fat, card sleeve (that's too big) but it looks so much better sitting on my record player.

I love the photo by [Macioce](https://www.instagram.com/maciocephotography/) used on the cover - a couple of years ago Macioce was kind enough to send me an 11x14" print of the photo which is hanging in _"the museum"_.
